My Trip To The Phillipines

Read Time: 5 minutes

Unforgettable Philippines: A Journey from Boracay to Cebu

The Philippines, with its pristine beaches, vibrant marine life, and adventurous landscapes, offers a treasure trove of experiences. My recent trip to this island paradise included a beautiful wedding on Boracay and thrilling adventures in Cebu. Here’s a detailed account of my journey, complete with highlights and must-see spots.

Day 1: Arrival in Boracay

My adventure began on the stunning island of Boracay, renowned for its powdery white sand and crystal-clear waters. The island’s White Beach, often hailed as one of the world’s most beautiful beaches, was the perfect introduction to this tropical paradise. I spent the afternoon relaxing by the beach, soaking in the sun, and indulging in some local delicacies from beachside vendors.

The main event of my Boracay visit was attending a wedding. The ceremony took place on top of the venue, with the turquoise waters and golden sunset providing a breathtaking backdrop. It was a magical experience, filled with joy, laughter, and the stunning natural beauty of Boracay.

Day 2: Exploring Boracay

The next day, I took the opportunity to explore more of Boracay’s offerings. I rented a kayak and paddled around the island, discovering secluded coves and hidden spots. For lunch, I enjoyed some freshly grilled seafood at a beachfront restaurant, savoring the flavors while enjoying the picturesque view.

In the afternoon, I took a trip to the nearby Puka Shell Beach, a quieter alternative to White Beach. The beach’s natural beauty and serene atmosphere provided a perfect retreat. I also visited the famous D’Mall, where I did some shopping and enjoyed the local nightlife.

Day 3: Journey to Cebu

After my relaxing stay in Boracay, I flew to Cebu, known for its rich history and stunning natural wonders.

Day 4: Canyoneering Adventure

One of the highlights of my Cebu visit was the exhilarating canyoneering experience in Badian. The adventure began with a thrilling trek through lush jungle, followed by a series of jumps, slides, and rappels down the turquoise waters of the Badian River. The adrenaline rush was unparalleled, and the natural scenery of waterfalls and rock formations made the experience even more memorable.

Day 5: Swimming with Whale Sharks

The final adventure of my Cebu trip was a once-in-a-lifetime experience: swimming with whale sharks in Oslob. These gentle giants, some measuring up to 40 feet long, gracefully glided through the waters, and I had the chance to get up close and personal with them. It was an awe-inspiring and humbling experience, seeing these magnificent creatures in their natural habitat.

Day 6: Departure

As my trip came to an end, I took some time to relax and reflect on the incredible experiences I had in the Philippines. I spent my final hours enjoying a leisurely breakfast and taking in the last views of Cebu before heading to the airport.

Final Thoughts

The Philippines truly is a paradise for travelers seeking both relaxation and adventure. From the idyllic beaches of Boracay to the thrilling adventures in Cebu, every moment was filled with wonder and excitement. Whether you’re attending a beautiful beach wedding, exploring underwater marvels, or embarking on thrilling canyoneering journeys, the Philippines offers an unforgettable experience.

Until next time, Philippines. Thank you for the incredible memories and the adventure of a lifetime!

"WAKI WAKI!" - Michael, 2024

My trip to Madeira

Read Time: 5 minutes

Exploring Madeira: A Journey Through Portugal's Island Gem

Nestled in the Atlantic Ocean, Madeira is often described as a floating garden. Known for its lush landscapes, rugged cliffs, and enchanting levadas, this Portuguese island offers a blend of natural beauty and cultural charm. I recently had the pleasure of exploring this verdant paradise, and I’m excited to share my itinerary and experiences with you.

Day 1: Arrival in Funchal

I landed in Madeira’s vibrant capital, Funchal, after a smooth flight. My first stop was the charming Mercado dos Lavradores. This bustling market is a sensory overload of colors and scents, showcasing a plethora of fresh produce, local fish, and the infamous Madeira wine. I picked up some tropical fruits and a bottle of wine to enjoy later.

In the afternoon, I wandered through the streets of Funchal, marveling at the blend of old-world charm and modernity. The Sé Catedral, a beautifully preserved 16th-century building, was a highlight. For dinner, I tried some traditional Madeiran cuisine at a local restaurant, savoring espetada (marinated beef skewers) and bolo do caco (garlic bread).

Day 2: Exploring the Levadas

No trip to Madeira would be complete without experiencing its famous levadas—irrigation channels that double as hiking trails. I embarked on the Levada do Caldeirão Verde hike. This trail led me through dense laurel forests and past cascading waterfalls, offering breathtaking views at every turn. The hike was moderately challenging but absolutely worth it for the scenery.

In the afternoon, I visited Santana, known for its traditional thatched-roof houses. These picturesque homes provide a glimpse into the island’s rural past and are perfect for some memorable photos.

Day 3: The Peaks of Madeira

My adventure continued with a trip to the island’s mountainous interior. I drove to Pico do Arieiro, one of Madeira’s highest peaks, where I was greeted by stunning panoramic views. The crisp, cool air and the sight of clouds floating below me were nothing short of magical.

From there, I made my way to Pico Ruivo, the highest point on the island. The hike between Pico do Arieiro and Pico Ruivo is renowned for its dramatic landscapes and challenging terrain. Although it was a tough climb, the sense of accomplishment and the views from the top were incredible.

Day 4: Discovering Câmara de Lobos and Cabo Girão

On the fourth day, I visited Câmara de Lobos, a quaint fishing village that Winston Churchill famously painted. The village’s harbor, filled with colorful boats and lively fish markets, was charming and picturesque. I enjoyed a leisurely lunch of fresh seafood at a local restaurant overlooking the harbor.

Next, I drove to Cabo Girão, one of the highest sea cliffs in Europe. The glass skywalk offered a thrilling view of the ocean far below, adding a touch of excitement to the day. The sheer drop and the sweeping views of the coastline were awe-inspiring.

Day 5: Relaxation and Departure

For my final day, I decided to take it easy. I strolled through the beautiful Madeira Botanical Garden, which showcases a diverse collection of plants from around the world. The garden’s tranquility was a perfect way to unwind before my departure.

In the afternoon, I returned to Funchal for a final stroll through the Old Town, exploring its charming streets and quaint shops. Before heading to the airport, I enjoyed a leisurely meal at a café, reflecting on the island’s beauty and my unforgettable experiences.

Final Thoughts

Madeira is a destination that captivates with its natural beauty and welcoming spirit. From the dramatic levadas and stunning peaks to the charming towns and rich culinary experiences, there’s something for every traveler. Whether you’re an adventure seeker or someone looking to relax in a breathtaking setting, Madeira offers a unique and memorable experience.

Until next time, Madeira. Thank you for the incredible memories!
